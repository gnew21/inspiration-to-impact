The Challenge

Most inspiration ends when a program finishes.

Each year, over 160,000 young people move through TYIF's programs, feeling inspired, empowered, and hopeful. Some leave with ideas for "changing the world" — solving real problems, creating genuine good. But most of that inspiration and potential dissipates the moment a program concludes.

Currently, there is no intentional, shared space that supports young people to continue developing ideas once a program ends. This model creates continuity — a structured pathway where ideas can be explored, tested, and where appropriate, scaled responsibly.

By 2030, TYIF will have reached more than 1,000,000 young people. The opportunity is not to push youth toward entrepreneurship — it's to intentionally channel the ideas, effort, and leadership that already exist.

01
Within TYIF · Open to all

TYIF Idea & Project Space

Phase 1 exists entirely within TYIF and serves as the primary engine for capturing and developing ideas. Embedded across TYIF brands, existing programs, alumni engagement and facilitator-led activities, it provides a structured mechanism for young people to articulate ideas, collaborate with others, and begin developing initiatives that respond to real needs and lived experience.

In this phase, there is no equity, no funding commitment, no IP transfer, and no commercial obligation. TYIF's role is to provide structure, feedback, continuity and direction. Every idea is acknowledged, categorised, and responded to.

Credibility is critical. Participants must see that ideas are taken seriously, responded to, and acted on where appropriate. Even small visible outcomes — program tweaks, pilot activities, facilitated collaborations — will demonstrate that this space is real.

Success in Phase 1 is measured by engagement, quality of ideas, tangible program improvements, and the extent to which young people invest sustained time and effort into developing their ideas.

Ideas that may emerge

  • Improvements to existing programs or delivery models
  • New initiatives aligned with TYIF's mission
  • Early-stage concepts that may eventually operate beyond TYIF

Enablers for success

  • Internal organisational structure and commitment
  • Consistent integration into programs and alumni pathways
  • Reliable processes for idea capture, review, and feedback

Phase 1 must avoid

  • Any implication of future commercialisation
  • Any transfer of ownership or IP
  • Any expectation of performance or outcomes
02
Within TYIF · Selective

TYIF Innovation Lab

Phase 2 is a progression for a small number of ideas that demonstrate the potential for deeper development. This phase remains entirely within TYIF and exists to test feasibility, build evidence, and assess real-world value.

Projects are piloted over a defined period — typically 8–12 weeks — with clear success metrics and boundaries. A small, clearly defined review group comprising TYIF leadership and relevant program representatives oversees progression and exit decisions.

Stopping a pilot should be framed as a successful learning outcome, not a failure. This protects resources, maintains focus, and reinforces that evidence drives progression.

Reaching the end of Phase 2 does not imply that a project should become a venture. Stopping at this point is a valid and successful outcome.

Entry criteria

  • Alignment with TYIF's mission
  • Clarity of the problem being addressed
  • Feasibility within TYIF's operating environment
  • Ethical safety

Possible outcomes

Phase 1 vs Phase 2

  • Phase 1 — broad, inclusive, capability-building; no outcome requirements
  • Phase 2 — selective, evidence-driven, decision-making environment
03
Outside TYIF via TCII · Very selective

External Venture Pathway via TCII

Phase 3 provides a deliberate, ethical, and operationally sound pathway for a very small number of projects to become independent ventures. All activity occurs outside The Youth Impact Foundation, via The Catalytic Impact Institution (TCII).

This separation is essential to protect TYIF's charitable status, youth safeguarding obligations, and governance integrity, while enabling legitimate commercial activity in an appropriate vehicle.

Participants formally transition from program participants to founders — moving from a developmental environment to one of full operational, legal, and financial accountability. This transition is always optional, deliberate, and opt-in.

TCII operates as a venture studio and risk-bearing partner — not an incubator, accelerator, or owner of ideas. Its role is to reduce execution risk and increase venture quality by providing hands-on support where early-stage founders typically lack capability.

Entry conditions (all must be met)

  • Phase 2 formally completed with documented outcomes
  • Clear case that project cannot or should not continue within TYIF
  • Individuals explicitly opt in as founders or collaborators
  • All participants are no longer TYIF program participants
  • Informed consent documented; independent legal advice encouraged

TCII disciplines

  • Highly selective — very few projects enter Phase 3
  • Patient — ventures built deliberately, not rushed
  • Quality-focused — no pressure for volume
04
Fully Independent

Scale, Sustainability, or Exit

Phase 4 begins once ventures are fully independent and operating in the market. Ventures are no longer connected to TYIF's program delivery or participant environment — they operate with full responsibility for their own commercial, legal, and operational decisions.

Financial value created through successful ventures is designed to flow back to TYIF through pre-agreed, transparent mechanisms. Over time, this creates a complementary sustainability pathway for TYIF, enabling reinvestment in programs, innovation, and long-term impact while reducing reliance on grants and donations.

Failure is an expected and acceptable outcome. Ventures that close responsibly still generate meaningful value in the form of leadership development, commercial experience, and system-level learning.

Venture pathways

  • Sustainable, profitable operation without external capital
  • Growth through angel or venture capital investment
  • Acquisition by a larger organisation
  • Orderly closure with learning retained

The Partner

The Catalytic Impact Institution

TCII operates as a venture studio and risk-bearing partner — not an incubator, accelerator, or owner of ideas. It provides structured support across five practical domains, absorbing execution risk so founders can focus on building.

TCII operates as an independent public company, entirely separate from TYIF, ensuring full protection of TYIF's charitable status.

Ownership & Value

Designed to be ethical, transparent, and fair.

🏗️

Founders keep control

TCII holds a minority, non-controlling equity stake in ventures it supports. Founders retain majority ownership and decision-making authority. Young people are never required to give up ownership simply for participation.

🛡️

TYIF stays protected

TYIF bears no operational, financial, or legal risk related to external ventures. TCII absorbs commercial, legal, and execution risk during venture formation. Venture outcomes — success or failure — do not compromise TYIF's programs or charitable obligations.

🔄

Value flows back

Financial value from successful ventures flows back to TYIF through TCII — via dividends, exit proceeds, grants, or strategic partnerships. This creates long-term sustainability, reducing reliance on grants and reinvesting in future programs.

Design Principles

What makes this model work.

Impact first, always

TYIF remains focused on people, impact, and program delivery at every stage. Commercial activity enters only through a separately structured vehicle, well after charitable obligations have been honoured.

Evidence drives progression

Advancement through phases is earned, not assumed. Clear criteria, time-bound pilots, and explicit exit decisions ensure progression is driven by real-world evidence rather than enthusiasm or informal influence.

Deliberate separation

The boundary between TYIF and commercial activity is clear and firm. Capital and commercial investment enter only after independent company formation, ensuring TYIF's charitable integrity is never compromised.

Stopping is success

Most ideas will — and should — remain in Phase 1 or 2. Closing a pilot responsibly is framed as a valid, successful outcome that generates learning and leadership development for the broader ecosystem.

Opt-in at every stage

Progression is always deliberate and participant-driven. Individuals explicitly choose to become founders. There is no pressure, assumption, or expectation of commercial outcomes from participation in TYIF programs.

Long-term sustainability

As ventures succeed, value returns to TYIF through ethical, transparent mechanisms. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle — youth-led ideas strengthening the organisation that made them possible in the first place.
